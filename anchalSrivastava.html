Dr. Anchal Srivastava earned his B.Sc. in Physics, Mathematics, and Chemistry from Purvanchal University, Jaunpur in 1992, and his M.Sc. in Physics from DDU Gorakhpur University in 1994. He completed his Ph.D. in Physics from the Department of Physics, Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi in 2002, with a research focus on the synthesis and characterization of carbon nanotubes. Over the years, he has established himself as a leading figure in the field of nanomaterials, with specific expertise in structural and spectroscopic studies of 0D (quantum dots), 1D (carbon nanotubes, nanorods, and nanoribbons), and 2D (graphene and transition metal dichalcogenides) materials. Currently a Professor in the Department of Physics at BHU, Dr. Srivastava has more than 17 years of experience in teaching undergraduate and postgraduate students, and has significantly contributed to academic administration. He has mentored over 15 Ph.D. students, including international scholars, and continues to supervise several others. His pioneering work spans across nanostructure synthesis, surface and interface characterization, microfabrication, AFM-based analysis, and device applications in optoelectronics, energy storage, and biosensing.

Dr. Srivastava has received numerous prestigious awards and recognitions for his contributions to science and technology. These include the Fellow of the International Academy of Nanotechnology (USA, 2022), Shri Sant Sanganeria Rotating Professorship (BHU, 2020), Most Productive Researcher Award (BHU, 2020), Vice Chancellors Award for Excellence in Research (BHU, 2014), and the Young Investigator Award at the World Congress on Biomimetics, Artificial Muscles and Nano-Bio (Korea, 2013). His international research engagements include the BOYSCAST Fellowship from DST-India, Max Planck-India Fellowship, and participation in the 55th Lindau Meeting of Nobel Laureates and Students (Germany, 2005). He has played a key role in multiple R&D projects funded by premier Indian agencies such as DST, including the development of 2D photodetectors, biosensors for cardiovascular disease, and carbon bucky paper-based cancer diagnostics. His work has led to the development and transfer of several technologies, resulting in granted and filed Indian and U.S. patents, including a notable U.S. patent on CNT filters. His contributions extend to several influential book chapters on nanomaterials, including topics like 2D materials for flexible electronics, CNT membrane filters, and nanocomposites in biomedical applications.

Dr. Srivastava’s interdisciplinary approach and collaborative research ethos continue to drive innovation in materials science, particularly in quantum materials, nanoscale device engineering, and sustainable nanotechnologies. His efforts aim to bridge fundamental physics with real-world applications, positioning him among India’s prominent academic figures in the rapidly evolving domain of nanoscience and nanotechnology.
